——— ⚙️ Kinematics ———

——— 🚗 One-Dimensional Motion ———

~ 📝 Definitions ~

  • Time (t):
    How long you were driving for. Unit: seconds (s)
  • Position (x):
    Where you are or where you were. Unit: meters (m)
  • Velocity (v):
    The way your position changes over time — speed with direction. Unit: meters per second (m/s)
  • Acceleration (a):
    The way your velocity changes over time. Unit: meters per second squared (m/s²)

~ 📐 Formulas ~

Average velocity:

$$\bar{v} = \frac{\Delta x}{\Delta t}$$

(Displacement divided by time interval)

Average acceleration:

$$\bar{a} = \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t}$$

(Change in velocity divided by time interval)

Final velocity:

$$v = v_0 + at$$

(Initial velocity plus acceleration times time)

Average velocity (constant acceleration):

$$\bar{v} = \frac{v_0 + v}{2}$$

Displacement under constant acceleration:

$$x - x_0 = v_0 t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2$$

~ 💡 Notes ~

For any physical variable that changes over time:

  • If it's constant, use the value directly.
  • If it varies, and only beginning and end are known:
    $$\bar{V} = \frac{V_0 + V_{\text{end}}}{2}$$
  • This is an effective approximation when the exact form is unknown.

——— ✈️ Two-Dimensional Motion ———

~ ➗ Vectors ~

  • Motion in 2D is described using vectors:
    $$\vec{r} = x \hat{i} + y \hat{j}$$
    (In 3D, add: \( z \hat{k} \))
  • Velocity vectors can be decomposed into components using trigonometry:
    If an object is launched at an angle (\( \theta = 30^\circ \)) with speed (\( v \)):
    • Horizontal component:
      $$v_x = v \cos(\theta)$$
    • Vertical component:
      $$v_y = v \sin(\theta)$$
  • Velocity vector:
    $$\vec{v} = v_x \hat{i} + v_y \hat{j} = v \cos(\theta) \hat{i} + v \sin(\theta) \hat{j}$$
  • Apply 1D motion equations independently in x and y directions.
